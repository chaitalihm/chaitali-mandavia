Keep in Touch — a friend tracker that nudges you
Live

I kept forgetting to check in with people I actually care about, so I built a small desktop app to fix that. Every friend gets a polaroid on a corkboard. Flip the card and you see when you last talked, whether it was a text or a call, and a quick note about what you talked about last. There's a "coming up" tab that surfaces birthdays and plans so nothing falls through the cracks.

The whole thing runs locally as an Electron app, no server, no account, your data stays on your machine. I wanted it to feel more like a physical corkboard than a CRM, which is why everything is hand-drawn and a little imperfect on purpose.

A fitness journal that talks back
Live

I wanted to log my workouts without typing anything. So I built a voice-first journal: you speak your morning routine and an LLM with guardrails extracts the structured fields it needs (what you did, how long, how you felt) and asks follow-up questions if something's missing. Once everything is captured, it confirms before saving anything.

The interesting part to build was the guardrails. A raw LLM will happily make up a duration if you don't mention one. I had to force it to explicitly ask for missing fields rather than guessing, and to show a "captured" percentage so you can see what's still needed before it saves.
